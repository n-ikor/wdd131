Nigeria

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is Africa's most populous nation with over 220 million people and more than 250 ethnic groups. Located in West Africa along the Gulf of Guinea, it features diverse landscapes from mangrove swamps in the south to savannas and semi-arid zones in the north. A former British colony that gained independence in 1960, Nigeria is a federal republic with Abuja as its capital and Lagos as its economic hub. It is the continent's largest economy, driven by oil, agriculture, and a booming tech and entertainment sector, including Nollywood. Rich in culture, history, and natural resources, Nigeria plays a pivotal role in African politics, peacekeeping, and global energy markets.
